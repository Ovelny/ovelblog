Broadcast domains and collision domains

History

  • Less frequent with full-duplex
  • "Collision" is kind of a misleading term

Analogy for an half-duplex connection: a conference call. Only one person must talk at a time or else no one can understand each other.

Back then, everything was on the same cable, with either no hub or a half-duplex hub. The solution was to use switches to separate collision domains.

Nowadays, there is no collision domain anymore: just one device per domain.

Broadcast domain

  • A necessary evil: for ARP requests, system notifications, etc
  • The scope of a broadcast domain is limited to routers, but goes through switches

When the topology is just made of one switch, everyone receives a broadcast message.
